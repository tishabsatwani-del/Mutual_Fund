How this works

Every reaction below buys into the exact same market — the same monthly SIP, the same single crash, the same recovery back to trend. We track it the honest way funds do: each month your money buys units at that month's price (NAV). During a crash, units are cheap, so staying invested quietly stockpiles them. The moment you sell and buy back later — at a higher price — you simply own fewer units forever. That gap never closes. That is the behavior gap.

The Regular plan never sells, but a small expense ratio leaks out every year and compounds into a surprisingly large "toll" over decades. Returns shown are money-weighted (XIRR). This is a model for understanding behavior, not financial advice or a prediction.
